Lynette

"Rainy days are perfect for spacing out..."

Lynette is the stoic half of the Twin Magicians of Fontaine. She avoids social interactions, letting her brother Lyney do the talking while she quietly watches while sipping tea.

Lynette is a run-of-the-mill Anemo support unit. She’s a free unit everyone obtains after reaching Adventure Rank 25. She’s incredibly beginner-friendly being easy to play and build, and can be slotted into many teams. Since she’s a jack-of-all, she’s a master-of-none.

Materials

Common Drop

Lynette uses the Gear family for her common drops. They’re obtained from any mechanical enemy found in Fontaine.

Local Specialty

The Lumidouce Bell is Lynette’s local specialty for her Ascension. They’re found on land and most plentiful to the north of the Court of Fontaine.

Talent Book

Lynette uses the Order family of Talent Books. They can be obtained on Wednesday, Saturday, and Sunday.

Normal Boss

Lynette uses the Coppelia version of the Artificed Space Clockwork Component. Be sure to select the correct version of the dance when initiating the Icewind Suite battle outside the Opera Epiclese.

Weekly Boss

Everamber is used for Lynette’s high-level Talent upgrades. It’s obtained for the Weekly Boss Guardian of Apep’s Oasis.

Talents

Normal Attack: Rapid Ritesword

Normal Attack: "Performs up to 4 rapid strikes."

Charged Attack: "Consumes a certain amount of Stamina to unleash 2 rapid sword strikes."

Plunging Attack: "Plunges from mid-air to strike the ground below, damaging opponents along the path and dealing AoE DMG upon impact."

Lynette’s Normal Attack is the basic sword 4-strike combo.

The Normal Attack can be completely ignored unless Lynette is C6 and you wish to use her as an on-field dps.

Elemental Skill: Enigmatic Feint

"Flicks her mantle and executes an Enigma Thrust, dealing Anemo DMG. When the Enigma Thrust hits an opponent, it will restore Lynette's HP based on her Max HP, and in the 4s afterward, she will lose a certain amount of HP per second. Based on whether you press or hold this ability, she will use Enigma Thrust differently."

"A maximum of 1 opponent can have Shadowsign at any one time. When this opponent gets too far from Lynette, the Shadowsign will be canceled."

"Arkhe: Ousia At specific intervals, Lynette will unleash a Surging Blade when she uses Enigma Thrust, dealing Ousia-aligned Anemo DMG."

Press

"She swiftly uses an Enigma Thrust."

Energy Generation: 4 particles

The Press version should be used most of the time because it is quicker to execute.

Hold

"Lynette will enter a high-speed Pilfering Shadow state and apply Shadowsign to a nearby opponent. You can control her movement direction during this state, and you can end it prematurely by using this skill again. When this high-speed state ends, Lynette will unleash her Enigma Thrust. If there is an opponent with Shadowsign applied to them nearby, Lynette will approach them in a flash before using Enigma Thrust."

Energy Generation: 4 particles

The only situations when to use the Hold version are when Lynette needs to chase down a target. Selecting a specific target within a crowd of enemies is difficult, so don’t waste time running in circles.

The Hold version is decent for quicker exploration. Although it’s very similar to Yelan’s Skill, Lynette’s is noticeably slower at around the speed of Alt Sprint characters like Mona and Ayaka.

A thing to note is Lynette’s built-in Health drain and heal. This mechanic can be generally ignored since it brings nothing to her kit other than just there as Fontaine’s gimmick.

Lynette’s Skill will be used often, but it can stay at lower levels since the majority of her team contribution comes from her Burst and Viridescent Venerere.

Elemental Burst: Magic Trick: Astonishing Shift

Energy Cost: 70 "Lynette raises her mantle high, dealing AoE Anemo DMG, using skillful sleight of hand to make a giant Bogglecat Box appear!"

Bogglecat Box:
·Taunts nearby opponents, attracting their attacks.
·Deals Anemo DMG to nearby opponents at intervals.
·When the Bogglecat Box comes into contact with Hydro/Pyro/Cryo/Electro, it will gain the corresponding element and additionally fire Vivid Shots that will deal DMG from that element at intervals.
Elemental Absorption of this kind will only occur once during this ability's duration.

Magic Trick: Astonishing Shift is Lynette’s most important aspect of her kit and is everything good for an Anemo support. It taunts, swirls, and applies another absorbed element.

Lynette’s Burst is her highest priority Talent, but despite that, it’s not as important as other core Talents to level up on other characters.

Overall Talent Priority

Burst (with extra resources) > Skill (when nothing else to work on) > Normal (only C6)

Lynette has very low Talent needs, which is great for new players. She can perform her role with all Talents at level 1, so only upgrade when extra resources are available.

Passive Talents

Loci-Based Mnemonics

"Shows the location of nearby Recovery Orbs on the minimap. The Aquatic Stamina and HP gained from touching Orbs will be increased by 25%."

Loci-Based Mnemonics is a unique Utility Passive only functional in the waters of Fontaine. Since underwater exploration and combat aren’t affected by character levels, slotting a Level 1 Lynette into your diving team will make diving more enjoyable.

Sophisticated Synergy

"Within 10s after using Magic Trick: Astonishing Shift, when there are 1/2/3/4 Elemental Types in the party, all party members' ATK will be increased by 8%/12%/16%/20% respectively."

Sophisticated Synergy is a simple team-wide Attack increase. Since Lynette is an Anemo swirl unit, she’ll likely be in a rainbow team with 3 other element types.

Props Positively Prepped

"After the Bogglecat Box summoned by Magic Trick: Astonishing Shift performs Elemental Conversion, Lynette's Elemental Burst will deal 15% more DMG. This effect will persist until the Bogglecat Box's duration ends."

Props Positively Prepped is kind of a wasted Passive since Lynette isn’t designed to deal much damage on her own.

Rotation

Skill -> Burst -> Swap

Lynette has a simple rotation. She takes the field after a character who applies an element to enemies and wants their element swirled, uses her Skill, uses her Burst, then swaps to the next character.

Constellation

Constellation 1: A Cold Blade Like a Shadow

"When Enigmatic Feint's Enigma Thrust hits an opponent with Shadowsign, a vortex will be created at that opponent's position that will pull nearby opponents in."

Lynette’s Constellation 1 introduces a welcomed Crowd Control aspect to her Skill. Just don’t expect much from it as it’s fairly weak compared to other Anemo pulls.

Constellation 2: Endless Mysteries

"Whenever the Bogglecat Box summoned by Magic Trick: Astonishing Shift fires a Vivid Shot, it will fire an extra Vivid Shot."

After unlocking Endless Mysteries, Lynette’s Burst will now fire 2 projectiles of the adsorbed element. This does not affect the Anemo damage in any way. It will target separate enemies if available.

Constellation 3: Cognition-Inverting Gaze

"Increases the Level of Magic Trick: Astonishing Shift by 3. Maximum upgrade level is 15."

Constellation 3 is the typical Burst upgrade.

Constellation 4: Tacit Coordination

"Increases Enigmatic Feint's charges by 1."

Tacit Coordination gives Lynette an extra charge of her Skill. This doesn’t change her playstyle but is nice for exploration.

Constellation 5: Obscuring Ambiguity

"Increases the Level of Enigmatic Feint by 3. Maximum upgrade level is 15."

Constellation 5 is the typical Skill upgrade.

Constellation 6: Watchful Eye

"When Lynette uses Enigmatic Feint's Enigma Thrust, she will gain an Anemo Infusion and 20% Anemo DMG Bonus for 6s."

Watchful Eye gives Lynette the option to be played as an on-field Anemo DPS.

It should be noted that she’s still better as an Anemo support and changing her to become a DPS greatly increases her build requirements. She’ll now need more Talent upgrade investments, and Attack and Crit stats on her artifacts. But for those that enjoy the character, this is a reason to play her on-field.

Constellation Final Thoughts

Overall, Lynette has decent Constellations, but nothing groundbreaking.

Weapons

5* Weapons

Elegy for the End

Base ATK: 608
Sub Stat: 55.1% Energy Recharge

The Parting Refrain: (R1) "A part of the "Millennial Movement" that wanders amidst the winds. Increases Elemental Mastery by 60. When the Elemental Skills or Elemental Bursts of the character wielding this weapon hit opponents, that character gains a Sigil of Remembrance. This effect can be triggered once every 0.2s and can be triggered even if said character is not on the field. When you possess 4 Sigils of Remembrance, all of them will be consumed and all nearby party members will obtain the "Millennial Movement: Farewell Song" effect for 12s. "Millennial Movement: Farewell Song" increases Elemental Mastery by 100 and increases ATK by 20%. Once this effect is triggered, you will not gain Sigils of Remembrance for 20s. Of the many effects of the "Millennial Movement," buffs of the same type will not stack."

Lynette has low weapon needs, so almost anything will work. If you have her at C6 and want to play her as an on-field DPS, give her any 5* weapon as a basic stat-stick.

Otherwise, Elegy for the End is her best 5* support weapon. It gives her an awesome chunk of Energy Recharge and furthers her team-boosting abilities.

4* Weapons

Favonius Warbow

Base ATK: 454
Sub Stat: 61.3% Energy Recharge

Windfall: (R5) "CRIT Hits have a 100% chance to generate a small amount of Elemental Particles, which will regenerate 6 Energy for the character. Can only occur once every 6s."

Lynette’s best 4* weapon is Favonius Warbow. It gives her Energy Recharge like Elegy, but instead of granting her team Elemental Mastery and Attack, she supports her team with additional energy.

Stringless

Base ATK: 510
Sub Stat: 165 Elemental Mastery

Arrowless Song: (R5) "Increases Elemental Skill and Elemental Burst DMG by 48%."

Stringless gives Lynette more personal damage and stronger swirls. But since she’s primarily a support with low damage, especially for those that haven’t invested much into her Talents, the extra damage isn’t worth it.

Windblume Ode

Base ATK: 510
Sub Stat: 165 Elemental Mastery

Windblume Wish: (R5) "After using an Elemental Skill, receive a boon from the ancient wish of the Windblume, increasing ATK by 32% for 6s."

Windblume Ode is Lynette’s best C6, on-field DPS 4* weapon. It gives her a nice chunk of Elemental Mastery for stronger swirls and gives a good Attack buff for the duration of her infusion.

Artifacts

Desired Stats

Sands of Eon

Energy Recharge%

Energy Recharge is the best Sands. Lynette is Burst-dependent, so getting her Burst as often as possible is more important than stronger swirls an EM piece could give.

Goblet of Eonothem

EM

An Elemental Mastery Goblet will make her swirls stronger, which is her large damage source.

Circlet of Logos

EM = Crit Rate%

If Favonius Warbow is equipped, use a Crit Rate% Circlet. If not, use Elemental Mastery. Having more reliable extra energy from Favonius Warbow is more important.

Sub Stats

Energy Recharge% > EM > Crit Rate% > Crit Dmg%

Lynette has very low stat requirements, making her excellent for new players. With her base kit, she only needs to focus on Energy Recharge and Elemental Mastery. If Favonius Warbow is used, try to get as much Crit Rate as possible.

Her stat requirements increase if C6 is unlocked and you wish to play her as an on-field DPS. Add Crit Dmg and Attack to her wanted sub-stats.

Viridescent Venerer

2-Pieces: "Anemo DMG Bonus +15%"
4-Pieces: "Increases Swirl DMG by 60%. Decreases opponent's Elemental RES to the element infused in the Swirl by 40% for 10s."

Regardless of the build, Viridescent Venerer is the one and only artifact set. The extra swirl damage and Resistance shred is too good to pass.

Teammates

Anemo Support

Lynette is an extremely flexible Anemo Support that can be slotted into any Reaction-based team. The only units she doesn’t work well with are Geo and Dendro, to a lesser extent. Other than that, almost every character available in Genshin will benefit from her on the team.

(C6) On-Field DPS

If you want to run her as the main on-field DPS, Lynette could be paired with Faruzan to boost her personal damage and other off-field supports or damage dealers like Bennett, Xiangling, or Xingqiu.
