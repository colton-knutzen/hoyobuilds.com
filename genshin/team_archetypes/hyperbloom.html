Hyperbloom

Hyperbloom is the reaction of Electro coming into contact with Dendro Cores transforming them into homing missiles that deal increased damage to the nearest target.

Hyperbloom requires a minimum of three elements- Dendro, Hydro, and Electro. The Electro unit needs to apply their element in an AoE. This makes the archetype less flexible. But in return, these teams are powerful and safer. Hyperbloom can’t explode and deal damage to teammates like Bloom or Burgeon does.

Hyperbloom has the unique feature of enemies maintaining three total elemental auras at once. Electro and Hydro from Electro-charged. And Dendro and Electro from Quicken.

In this sense, Hyperbloom archetype teams aren’t just Hyperbloom. They will always have access to Quicken, Bloom, Electro-charged, and Hyperbloom.

Disclaimer

The example teams are not intended to be the best for that specific archetype. Some of them are strong, while others are just fun teams that will struggle in the highest levels of the Abyss. Some archetypes are weaker than others, as they don’t have a strong roster to draw from. But that doesn’t make them less enjoyable to play in the overworld.

We tried restricting each team to no more than two limited 5* units.

All the archetypes have multiple lineups that work. Use these guides to help you experiment with your own teams!

The basic Hyperbloom archetype uses the three required Elements with a 4th flex character of any of those elements. Which element greatly depends on the team, but any are viable.

Alhaitham Hyperbloom (Example Team)

Alhaitham Hyperbloom is a very powerful team. Yelan applies a ton of off-field Hydro, good sub-dps, and increases the damage of the on-fielder’s normal attacks.

Kuki provides great healing, good AoE electro application to trigger Hyperblooms, and is built with EM. This allows her Hyperblooms to deal even more damage.

Nahida applies powerful amounts of Dendro, great sub-dps, and boosts all of the team’s reaction damage.

Alhaitham is a powerful on-field unit in himself, but he’s able to drive both Nahida and Yelan for even greater results.

Yelan Nahida Kuki Alhaitham

Thunderstorm

Thunderstorm replaces the 4th flex unit with Anemo. The Anemo unit causes a chain reaction of reactions with their Swirls, and reduces the resistance of the swirled element. Anemo doesn’t react with Dendro, and since Hydro has a higher priority than Electro, the swirled element will likely be Hydro. You’ll want to consider a Hydro main DPS to capitalize on that.

The Ancients (Example Team)

The Ancients team uses Nahida as the off-field Dendro unit, sub-dps, and reaction buffer.

Raiden is the Hyperbloom trigger with her wide AoE Skill.

Venti gathers enemies and reduces the Hydro resistance.

Neuvillette is the main on-fielder Hydro applicator.

This team will want Nahida to go first, then Neuvillette, Venti, and then Raiden before going back to Neuvillette. This way Venti will Swirl the Hydro element, allowing Neuvillette’s charged attacks to deal even more damage.

Neuvillette Nahida Raiden Venti

Hyperfridge

Hyperfridge is a team that takes advantage of Dendro and Cryo not reaction to one another. Hyperfridge has a ton of reactions at its disposal:
  • Frozen with Hydro and Cryo
  • Bloom with Hydro and Dendro
  • Superconduct with Cryo and Electro
  • Electro-charged with Hydro and Electro
  • Hyperbloom with Electro and Bloom
  • Quicken with Electro and Dendro

Not all of these reactions will occur in every Hyperfridge team. And some will be more prominent than others. Which ones reign supreme depends on the teams’ composition and how it’s played.

Cyno's Hyperfridge of Puns (Example Team)

Cyno’s Hyperfridge of Puns has Xingqiu as the Hydro applicator, some healing, and sub-dps.

Nahida boosts the teams’ reactions and applies a ton of off-field Dendro and damage.

Ganyu is used for her Burst to apply Cryo in a wide area.

And Cyno is the main DPS and on-fielder, driving Xingqiu and Nahida, and triggering Hyperblooms.
