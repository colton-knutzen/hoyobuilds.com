"This is my original appearance, that of the Vidyadhara... Don't worry, I'm still Dan Heng, a descendant of the Vidyadhara, and also a member of the Nameless."
Lore Factoid
Imbibitor Luane is a latin phrase meaning "Drinking under the Moonlight". The moon is reflected upon one's cup, and taking a sip is like drinking the moon itself. It's interesting his title isn't a tree-related metaphore, since the Luofu's plaguemark is a tree while the Yaoging's is a moon.

Dan Heng - Imbibitor Lunae. Dan Feng. Daniel. Dan Heng 2. Neuvillette expy. Whatever you call him, Imbibitor Luane is the glow-up we’ve all been waiting for since the Interstellar Journey trailer at the game’s launch.

Imbibitor Lunae is the same Dan Heng we’ve been traveling with on the Express, just with the High Elder powers he’s kept hidden.

Imbibitor Lunae is a powerful AoE Imaginary damage dealer with two playstyles:

Slow and Powerful; unleashing his strongest attack every turn at the cost of 3 Skill Points.

Fast and Balanced; alternating turns with Basic Attack and his strongest attack.

Imbibitor Lunae is a pure damage dealer. He has no supportive abilities or self-survival capabilities, despite following the Path of Destruction. He has some of the highest damage potential in the game, but he needs a team dedicated to keeping him alive and supplementing Skill Points.

Materials

Common Drop

The Immortal family is used for Imbibitor Lunae’s Common Drops. They’re obtained by defeating his lifelong enemies; creatures of the Abundance.

Calyx Material

Imbibitor Lunae uses the basic Destruction Calyx materials for his Skills and Trace upgrades. They’re obtained in the Storage Zone on Herta Space Station.

Stagnant Shadow

Imbibitor Lunae uses the new Stagnant Shadow boss released in version 1.3. It’s found in the Artisanship Commission.

Echo of War

Regret of Infinite Ochema is used for Imbibitor Lunae’s high-level Trace upgrades. This drops from the Echo of War in Scalegorge Waterscape.

Skills & Traces

Basic Attack: Beneficent Lotus

"Uses a 2-hit attack and deals Imaginary DMG equal to 100% of Dan Heng • Imbibitor Lunae's ATK to a single enemy target." (Lv.6)

Energy Gain: 20

Toughness Break: 30

The only special thing about the Basic Attack is that it’s a 2-hit combo. This will be covered later.

Imbibitor Lunae’s Enhanced Basic Attacks scale with the Basic Attack level. This makes it the most important Trace to level up. Max it out before the rest.

Skill: Dracore Libre

"Enhances Basic ATK. Enhancements may be applied up to 3 times consecutively. Using this ability does not consume Skill Points and is not considered as using a Skill.
Enhancing Basic ATK "Beneficent Lotus" 1 time transforms Basic ATK into "Transcendence."
Enhancing Basic ATK "Beneficent Lotus" 2 times transforms Basic ATK into "Divine Spear."
Enhancing Basic ATK "Beneficent Lotus" 3 times transforms Basic ATK into "Fulgurant Leap."
When unleashing Divine Spear or Fulgurant Leap, 1 stack of "Outroar" is gained before every hit (starting from the fourth hit). Each stack of "Outroar" increases Dan Heng • Imbibitor Lunae's CRIT DMG by 12.0%, for a max of 4 stacks. These stacks last until the end of his turn." (Lv.10)

Energy Gain: 0

Toughness Break: 0

Imbibitor Lunae’s Skill enhances his Basic Attack into 1 to 3 levels depending on how many Skill Points are consumed. The only other important note is that it increases the Crit DMG for levels 2 & 3.

Upgrading the Skill only increases the Crit DMG granted to a level 2 or 3 Enhanced Basic Attack. The improvement isn’t much, so the Skill isn’t as important to upgrade.

Lore Factoid
Dracore is actually a fictional word but clearly has Dragon influences. Libre is Spanish for “liberty”. Making Dracore Libre mean something like “The Liberated Dragon”.

Enhanced Basic Attack Lv. 1: Transcendence

"Unleashes 3 hits in this attack and deals Imaginary DMG equal to 260% of Dan Heng • Imbibitor Lunae's ATK to a single enemy target." (Lv.10)

Energy Gain: 30

Toughness Break: 60

Transcendence is barely better than the Basic Attack when compared with Skill Point costs. Gaining a Skill Point is better than consuming a Skill Point for this minor damage improvement. Never use Level 1 unless a little more damage is needed to end the battle against one last enemy.

Enhanced Basic Attack Lv. 2: Divine Spear

"Unleashes a 5-hit attack, dealing Imaginary DMG equal to 380% of Dan Heng • Imbibitor Lunae's ATK to a single enemy target. From the fourth hit onward, simultaneously deals Imaginary DMG equal to 60% of Dan Heng • Imbibitor Lunae's ATK to adjacent targets." (Lv.10)

Energy Gain: 35

Toughness Break: Target - 90 / Adjacent - 30

Again, Divine Spear is barely better than the Level 1 version when compared with Skill Point costs. Save the two Skill Points for either other teammates or Imbibitor Lunae’s next turn. Level 2 should only be used to end a fight.

Enhanced Basic Attack Lv. 3: Fulgurant Leap

"Unleashes a 7-hit attack, dealing Imaginary DMG equal to 500% of Dan Heng • Imbibitor Lunae's ATK to a single enemy target. From the fourth hit onward, simultaneously deal Imaginary DMG equal to 180% of Dan Heng • Imbibitor Lunae's ATK to adjacent targets." (Lv.10)

Energy Gain: 40

Toughness Break: Target - 120 / Adjacent - 60

Fulgurant Leap is Imbibitor Lunae’s most powerful attack and should be used the most often. It gives him the highest damage and toughness reduction per Skill Point, and the most energy per turn. If 3 Skill Points aren’t available at the start of his turn, use his Basic Attack. It’s better to wait than waste Skill Points on Level 1 or 2.

Azure’s Aqua Ablutes All does a similar amount of damage to a Level 2 Enhanced Basic Attack. The largest boon is that it not only avoids the 2 Skill Points cost but gives Imbibitor Lunae the equivalent of 2 Skill Points.

These extra Skill Points give supporting teammates opportunities to use their own Skills. Especially for the Slow and Powerful playstyle. They can set up buffs, defenses, or heals without encroaching on Imbibitor Lunae’s Skill Point needs. Just remember to save 1 Skill Point before his next turn.

Imbibitor Lunae’s Ultimate hits decently hard and benefits from upgrades. But it has a high cost of 140 energy, so it’ll only be used once every three turns at most.

Lore Factoid
There are 5 High Elders of the Vidyadhara spread across 5 Xianzhou Ships. They’re dragons in human form descended from the Aeon, Long the Permanence. The Ultimate gets its name from this as Imbibitor Lunae is known as the Azure Dragon, Commander of the Clouds and Rain.

Talent: Righteous Heart

"Dan Heng • Imbibitor Lunae gains 1 stack of Righteous Heart after each hit unleashed during an attack. Righteous Heart increases his DMG by 10.0%, up to 6 stacks. These stacks last until the end of his turn." (Lv.10)

Similar to Imbibitor Lunae’s Skill, Righteous Heart gives extra damage depending on the number of hits in the attack. It’s important to note that the buff only takes effect after the 1st hit, not before like the Skill’s Crit DMG buff. For example, the Basic Attack is a 2-hit combo, meaning only the 2nd hit will gain a 10% DMG buff while the 1st gains nothing. Every preceding hit will gain 10% more damage than the previous.

Similar to the Skill, upgrading this only increases the DMG% buff. The increase isn’t significant, but it’s still important to level-up.

Overall Talent Priority

Basic Attack >> Ultimate > Skill = Talent

Because Imbibitor Lunae is a powerful damage dealer, and all his Trace upgrades increase his damage, it’s very important to get them as high as possible.

Technique: Heaven-Quelling Prismadrakon

"After using his Technique, Dan Heng • Imbibitor Lunae enters the Leaping Dragon state for 20 seconds. While in the Leaping Dragon state, using his attack causes him to move forward rapidly for a set distance, attacking all enemies he touches and blocking all incoming attacks. After entering combat via attacking enemies in the Leaping Dragon state, Dan Heng • Imbibitor Lunae deals Imaginary DMG equal to 120% of his ATK to all enemies, and gains 1 Squama Sacrosancta."

Energy Gain: 0

Toughness Break: 60

Heaven-Quelling Prismadrakon is an initiating Technique. Entering battle while it’s active is required to gain the bonuses. Swapping characters will cancel the state and waste a Technique Point. Luckily, Imbibitor Lunae’s Technique is quite good. It deals a surprising amount of initial damage and gives the equivalent of 1 extra Skill Point.

Lore Factoid
Prismadrakon is another fictional word, but Prisma is Greek for “something shaped”. Making Heaven-Quelling Prismadrakon meaning something like, “Quelling the Heavens in the Shape of a Dragon.”

Eidolon

Eidolon 1: Tethered to Sky

"Increases the stackable Righteous Heart count by 4, and gains 1 extra stack of Righteous Heart for each hit during an attack."

Imbibitor Lunae’s first Eidolon essentially doubles the buff granted by his Talent. You can check the table’s “Talent DMG Bonus” column above if you’re curious how much E1 will grant each attack.

Eidolon 2: Imperium On Cloud Nine

"After using his Ultimate, Dan Heng • Imbibitor Lunae's action is Advanced Forward by 100% and gains 1 extra Squama Sacrosancta."

Imperium On Cloud Nine is huge. If used correctly, Imbibitor Lunae can perform a combo of Level 3 -> Ultimate -> Level 3 all within the same turn. This is his best Eidolon and stopping point.

Eidolon 3: Clothed in Clouds

"Skill Lv. +2, up to a maximum of Lv. 15. Basic ATK Lv. +1, up to a maximum of Lv. 10."

Eidolon 3 is the simple Basic Attack and Skill upgrade.

Eidolon 4: Zephyr's Bliss

"The buff effect granted by Outroar lasts until the end of this character's next turn."

When Imbibitor Lunae is played correctly, Zephyr’s Bliss gives him 100% uptime of max stacks on the Crit DMG% granted by his Skill, to every hit in all his attacks.

Eidolon 5: Fall is the Pride

"Ultimate Lv. +2, up to a maximum of Lv. 15. Talent Lv. +2, up to a maximum of Lv. 15."

Eidolon 5 is the traditional Ultimate and Talent upgrade.

Eidolon 6: Reign, Returned

"After any other ally uses their Ultimate, the Imaginary RES PEN of Dan Heng • Imbibitor Lunae's next Fulgurant Leap attack increases by 20%. This effect can stack up to 3 time(s)."

Resistance Penetrations are rare. Being able to ignore 60% of an enemy’s imaginary resistance is huge for a powerhouse like Imbibitor Lunae. But it’s not game-changing like E2.

Lore Factoid
The Vidyadhara rebirth process is undergone within special eggs. These pink eggs can be seen throughout the Scalegorge Waterscape map. Imbibitor Lunae's 6th Eidolon art depicts him within this egg during his forced rebirth.

Eidolon Final Thoughts

Imbibitor Lunae has incredible Eidolons. They all improve what he’s best at; dealing tremendous amounts of damage. Imperium On Cloud Nine is by far his best Eidolon. Being able to unleash a free Level 3 attack right after an Ultimate will wipe the field clean.

Relics & Ornament

Desired Stats

Body

Main Stat: Crit Rate% > Crit Dmg%

Imbibitor Lunae needs a minimum of 70% Crit Rate to use his best Relic set. Go with a Crit Dmg% Body piece if this minimum has been reached with Traces, Lightcones, Relics, and Substats. Otherwise, use Crit Rate%.

Feet

Main Stat: ATK% or SPD

Use Atk% Boots if going for the Slow and Powerful playstyle. Use SPD Boots if going for the Fast and Balanced playstyle.

Planar Sphere

Main Stat: Imaginary Dmg%

Imaginary Dmg% is the best option. Atk% can be used if one isn’t available in the Relic Set of choice.

Link Rope

Main Stat: ATK%

Atk% Rope is the best option since Imbibitor Lunae won’t gain his Ultimate in 2 turns even if wearing an Energy Recharge%.

Sub Stats

Speed Target: Any (ATK Boots) or 134 (SPD Boots)
Crit Rate% (minimum 70%) > SPD (SPD Boots) > Crit Dmg% > ATK%

Hitting the 70% Crit Rate minimum should be the first goal. This will allow him to capitalize on his built-in Crit Dmg% buff and take advantage of the Rutilant Arena set bonus.

If SPD Boots are used, then reaching 134 speed is the next goal. If Atk% boots are used, don’t worry about speed at all.

After those two goals have been reached, focus on maximizing Crit Dmg% and Atk%.

Wastelander of Banditry Desert

2pc Effect: " Increases Imaginary DMG by 10%."
4pc Set Effect: "When attacking debuffed enemies, the wearer's CRIT Rate increases by 10%, and their CRIT DMG increases by 20% against Imprisoned enemies."

Wastelander of Banditry Desert is Imbibitor Lunae’s best Relic set. It further encourages his match-up against Imaginary weak enemies, but it comes with some conditions. The only debuff Imbibitor Lunae can personally apply is Imprisoned when he weakness breaks. Therefore, a debuffer will likely be needed as a teammate. Luckily, Imbibitor Lunae has incredibly high Toughness Reduction values, and Imprisoned is used for the Crit Dmg% bonus this set provides.

Musketeer of Wild Wheat

2pc Effect: "ATK increases by 12%."
4pc Set Effect: "The wearer's SPD increases by 6% and Basic ATK DMG increases by 10%."

Musketeer of Wild Wheat is a great secondary set for those who don’t want to farm a dedicated Wastelander set. It’s better for the Fast and Balanced build, but less for the Slow and Powerful.

Rutilant Arena

2pc Effect: "Increases the wearer's CRIT Rate by 8%. When the wearer's current CRIT Rate reaches 70% or higher, the wearer's Basic ATK and Skill DMG increase by 20%."

Rutilant Arena is the best Planar set. It gives him a nice 8% Crit Rate and increases all non-Ultimate damage by 20%.

Space Sealing Station

2pc Effect: "Increases the wearer's ATK by 12%. When the wearer's SPD reaches 120 or higher, the wearer's ATK increases by an extra 12%."

Space Sealing Station is a decent set, but it will require a minimum of 120 speed, making it better for the Fast and Balanced build.

Teammates

Mono Imaginary

Imbibitor Lunae gains several buffs when going up against Imaginary weak enemies. From his Trace, Wastelander of Banditry Desert relic set, and On the Fall of an Aeon Lightcone. Building a mono-Imaginary team with Imbibitor Lunae as the main damage dealer will be a powerful force against Imaginary weak enemies.

The downside is the lack of options. Currently, the only other Imaginary units are Luocha, Welt, and Yukong. Luocha is a limited 5*, Welt is a permanent 5*, and Yukong doesn’t have the best synergy with Imbibitor Lunae since she has high Skill Point demands as well. Silver Wolf can be subbed into this team, but she is also a limited 5*.

Skill Point Gen Team

Imbibitor Lunae requires a tremendous amount of Skill Points, especially for the Slow and Powerful build. Some of the best Skill Point-positive teammates include Silver Wolf, Luocha, Pela, Fu Xuan, Bailu, and Tingyun.

In the future, there might even be some units that revolve around Skill Point generation. Dan Heng - Imbibitor Lunae will welcome them as a core teammate with open arms.
